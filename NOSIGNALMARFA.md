## Brand & Identity

### Name
**No Signal Marfa**

The name plays on the idea of disconnecting — going somewhere without cell service, without noise, without the feed. Marfa is famous for that. The irony: the house has gigabit fiber. Lean into it.

### Taglines (approved)
- *No WiFi. (Just kidding. Gigabit fiber.)*
- *A little house at the end of the highway. West Texas light. Room to breathe.*
- *No Signal. Just Marfa.*

### Voice & Tone

**The short version:** Dry, confident, specific. Sounds like a person, not a listing.

**Core principles:**

1. **Dry, self-aware wit** — The fiber joke is the template: *"No WiFi. (Just kidding. Gigabit fiber.)"* Acknowledge the irony, don't hide from it. The name is a contradiction — own it.

2. **Editorial, not salesy** — Describe the space and experience honestly. Trust the reader to want it. Never say "perfect for" or "ideal for" or "you'll love." Show, don't pitch.

3. **Specific over generic** — "Clerestory windows that flood the space with West Texas light" not "lots of natural light." "Cedar gate with a wall-mounted bottle opener" not "charming outdoor details." Details are what make people feel like they've already been there.

4. **Unhurried** — Don't bullet-point everything. Let sentences breathe. A paragraph that lingers is more on-brand than a crisp feature list. Pacing matches Marfa itself.

5. **Confident without being precious** — This is a cool, well-designed house. State that plainly. Don't oversell, but don't undersell either. It doesn't need exclamation points.

6. **West Texas literate** — Reference the landscape, the light, the silence, the scale. Marfa's allure is mostly about what *isn't* there. Lean into that.

**Words and phrases that fit the brand:**
- West Texas light / that light / the light
- Silence, stillness, unhurried
- The end of the highway / the middle of nowhere / the desert
- Exposed, polished, built with care
- Walk to everything
- Stars, sky, landscape, scale
- Disconnect, breathe, slow down

**Words and phrases to avoid:**
- "Perfect for..." / "Ideal for..."
- "Cozy" (overused, wrong vibe — this place is airy and architectural, not cozy)
- "Charming" (too soft)
- "Nestled" (never)
- "Luxurious" / "luxury" (implied, not stated)
- Exclamation points
- Emojis in formal copy
- "Amenity-rich" or any corporate rental language
- Anything that sounds like an Airbnb auto-description

**Example: Wrong vs. Right**

> ❌ "This charming studio is perfect for couples and families looking for a cozy retreat! Nestled in the heart of Marfa, you'll love the luxurious finishes and amazing sunsets!"

> ✅ "A purpose-built studio in the heart of Marfa. Exposed beam ceilings, polished concrete floors, and the kind of light that makes you want to stay an extra day."

### Writing Instructions for AI Assistants

When generating content for No Signal Marfa — whether website copy, Instagram captions, email responses, listing descriptions, or anything else — follow these rules:

**Always read this document first** if you haven't already. It is the source of truth.

**Tone checklist before publishing anything:**
- [ ] Does it sound like a person wrote it, not a template?
- [ ] Is it specific? (names, materials, details)
- [ ] Does it avoid the banned phrases above?
- [ ] Is it appropriately dry/witty without trying too hard?
- [ ] Would someone who reads Kinfolk or stays at Ace Hotel feel at home with this language?

**For website copy:** Follow the existing sections and hierarchy in `index.html`. Don't add sections without asking. Edits should feel like they were always there.

**For Instagram captions:** Short and understated. One or two sentences max. Let the photo do the work. A dry observation beats a description. Tag location as Marfa, TX. Use #marfa #marfatexas #westtexas #nosignalmarfa — keep hashtags minimal and at the end.

**For email responses to guests:** Warm but efficient. First-name basis. Confirm details clearly (dates, rate, deposit amount). Sign off as "No Signal Marfa" or just the owner's first name.

**For Airbnb/Vrbo listings (if used):** Adapt the website copy but follow platform conventions. The title should lead with the most compelling hook — something like "No Signal Marfa — Designer Studio, Walk Everywhere." Don't just copy/paste the website — those platforms reward keyword density in ways the website doesn't need.

**For any new feature or amenity added:** Update this document first, then the website, then any platform listings. Keep everything in sync.

## About Marfa (copy reference)

Marfa is a tiny West Texas ranch town (population ~1,700) that became one of the most quietly electrifying places in America — equal parts art world and cowboy country. It's famous for the Chinati Foundation (Donald Judd's large-scale permanent installation), Prada Marfa (the art installation/fake store on the highway), the Marfa Lights, and a thriving food and bar scene that punches far above its weight.

The draw: the scale of the landscape, the quality of the light, the silence, the stars. People drive 200+ miles into the Chihuahuan Desert to get here. They always come back.

**Nearby:**
- Chinati Foundation — 1 mile
- Hotel Paisano — 0.5 miles  
- Prada Marfa — 37 miles
- McDonald Observatory, Fort Davis — 60 miles
- Big Bend National Park — 3 hours
